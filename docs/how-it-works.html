The 2026 rules, in plain English

First World Cup with 48 teams. The format is new, the math is cruel, and someone in your group chat is confidently wrong about it. Here's the whole thing.

Who advances from the group stage?

32 of the 48 teams. There are 12 groups (A–L) of four. From each group, the winner and the runner-up advance automatically — that's 24. The remaining 8 spots go to the best third-placed teams: all 12 thirds are thrown into one ranking table, and the top 8 survive.

How are the 12 third-placed teams ranked?

  1. Points
  2. Goal difference
  3. Goals scored
  4. Fair play points (yellow/red cards)
  5. Drawing of lots — yes, really

This is the part nobody can do in their head: a third-placed team in Group A with 4 points might advance while a third in Group K with 4 points goes home on goal difference — and that comparison shifts with every goal scored in any of the 12 groups. That's literally why this site exists.

Tiebreakers inside a group

If two or more teams finish level, FIFA applies, in order:

  1. Points
  2. Goal difference (all group games)
  3. Goals scored (all group games)
  4. Points in the matches between the tied teams
  5. Goal difference between the tied teams
  6. Goals scored between the tied teams
  7. Fair play points
  8. Drawing of lots

Our calculator applies steps 1–6 exactly and shows a ⚖ icon when teams are so level that only fair play or lots (steps 7–8) could split them — we don't track yellow cards, and frankly neither does your uncle.

Key dates

  • June 11–27: group stage, 72 matches. Final round per group June 24–27 with simultaneous kickoffs.
  • June 28 – July 3: Round of 32 — the new knockout round the thirds fight to reach.
  • July 19: final at MetLife Stadium, New Jersey.
