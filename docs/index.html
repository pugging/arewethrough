    Why this exists

    The 2026 World Cup is the first with 48 teams, and the group-stage math got genuinely weird: finishing third in your group might be enough — but only if your record beats the third-placed teams of eleven other groups. During the last round of games (June 24–27) the permutations change with every goal, everywhere, at once.

    Quick answers

    How many teams advance from the group stage?

    32 of 48: the top two from each of the 12 groups, plus the 8 best third-placed teams.

    How are the best thirds ranked?

    Points, then goal difference, then goals scored. Still level? Fair play points, then a literal drawing of lots.

    What breaks ties inside a group?

    Points → goal difference → goals scored → head-to-head among the tied teams (points, GD, goals) → fair play → lots. This calculator applies the full chain and shows a scale icon when it comes down to fair play or lots.

    Is this official?

    No — it's an unofficial free fan tool, not affiliated with FIFA or any federation. Results refresh automatically from public sources during the tournament.
